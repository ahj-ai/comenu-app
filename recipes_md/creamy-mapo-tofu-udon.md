# Creamy Mapo Tofu Udon

A simple and spicy mapo tofu version served with udon noodles and a unique creamy tofu-peanut butter sauce.

**Macros:** Not specified

## Ingredients
- Ground beef or pork
- Minced garlic
- Scallions
- Doubanjiang (spicy bean paste)
- Chinese cooking wine (or mirin substitute)
- Black vinegar
- Black pepper (or sichuan peppercorn powder)
- Paprika
- Silken tofu
- Peanut butter
- Udon
- Chili crisp

## Steps
1. Mince garlic and chop scallions, separating the green and white parts.
2. In a lightly oiled pan, cook garlic and white scallions on medium heat.
3. Add doubanjiang (about 2-3 tbsp per lb of meat). Add in ground pork and cook til it fully browns.
4. Add in cooking wine, black vinegar, black pepper, and paprika to taste. Set aside.
5. In a pot cook udon for 2 min in boiling water, strain and set aside.
6. Drain tofu from package and blend with 1 tbsp of peanut butter (add a bit of water for smoothness). Set aside.
7. Plate by placing in udon first, then your meat and chopped green scallions then tofu cream.
8. Top with chili crisp and enjoy!
